Physiotherapy

Physiotherapy may support mobility, strength, function, injury recovery, pain management, and return to daily or recreational activity.

Common reasons for booking may include:

  • Muscle and joint pain
  • Back or neck discomfort
  • Sports injuries
  • Post-surgical rehabilitation
  • Reduced mobility
  • Workplace injuries
  • Motor vehicle accident injuries

What to expect:

An initial visit involves a thorough assessment to understand your concerns and movement patterns. Based on this, a practitioner may propose a personalized treatment approach.

Note: Treatment depends on an individual assessment and may vary based on your specific condition.

Massage Therapy

Massage therapy may support muscle relaxation, mobility, circulation, and management of muscular tension or discomfort.

Common reasons for booking may include:

  • General muscular tension and stiffness
  • Stress management
  • Post-workout recovery
  • Postural discomfort

What to expect:

During an initial visit, the massage therapist will discuss your health history and goals. They will then assess the soft tissues and joints of your body to propose an appropriate massage therapy plan.

Note: Massage therapy is intended to support overall well-being and mobility but is not claimed to cure medical conditions. Treatment depends on an individual assessment.

Chiropractic Care

Chiropractic care may focus on the assessment and management of musculoskeletal concerns, including joint movement, posture, back discomfort, and neck discomfort.

Common reasons for booking may include:

  • Neck or lower back pain
  • Sciatica and nerve-related discomfort
  • Headaches associated with neck tension
  • Joint stiffness and movement limitations

What to expect:

The initial visit includes a detailed medical history and a physical examination of the spine and joints. The chiropractor will discuss the findings and propose a tailored care plan.

Note: Treatment depends on an individual assessment. We prioritize evidence-based, responsible care pathways.

Naturopathy

Naturopathic care is an individualized, whole-person approach that may include lifestyle, nutrition, and wellness guidance within the practitioner’s professional scope.

What to expect:

An initial visit often involves a comprehensive discussion about your lifestyle, diet, stressors, and health history. The naturopath will work with you to develop a wellness plan.

Note: Naturopathy is complementary to, and does not replace, standard medical care provided by your family physician.

Acupuncture

Acupuncture may be used as part of a treatment plan for pain management, muscular tension, and general wellness, depending on assessment and suitability.

What to expect:

If deemed suitable, fine needles are inserted at specific points on the body. A practitioner will explain the process and ensure your comfort throughout the session.

Note: Treatment depends on an individual assessment and comfort level.

Psychotherapy

Psychotherapy provides a confidential setting to discuss emotional, behavioural, and mental well-being concerns with an appropriately qualified professional.

Common reasons for booking may include:

  • Stress and anxiety management
  • Coping with life transitions
  • Emotional distress related to injuries or accidents
  • Depression or low mood
